Lengua Machete explores orality as one of the central tools—and weapons—of Black peoples in their struggles for liberation.

The word emerges not only as a means to name pain and injustice, but as a force capable of cutting, beheading, and dismantling imposed truths—without filter, decorum, or restraint.

Through the dance of Ogun, ritual aesthetics, and performative action, the body-word invokes, enunciates, and denounces the truths that demand release, activating the voice as a machete: an ancestral, political, and spiritual blade.

**Machete for the Macho**

**Direction and creation:** Le'Onyx Da'Vida
